Resources

Voice

Direct, calm, second-person. French-accented English: friendly without being chirpy. Microcopy below a title explains the job, not the feature.

Page titles do the job

Pattern

Your prospects

Your tracking templates

Your fitness programs

Why

Possessive ("Your") makes the workspace feel owned. Plural noun, no verb.

Subtitles say what to do

Good

Manage and share your files with your clients.

Save time by creating your circuit, session and sports program templates.

Avoid

Welcome to the drive feature where you can store files.

A flexible templating system for power users.

CTAs are verbs

Pattern

Create a team · Add a payment · Create my coach profile

Why

Use the article. "Add a payment" reads better than "Add payment" — Ekklo's French roots show.

Empty states use the mascot

Pattern

No teams

Create your first team to start collaborating with other coaches.

Why

Two-line empty state: terse headline (no period) + one sentence of "first team" framing. The kettlebell mascot replaces the icon when the state is large.
